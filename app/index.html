Why This Exists

This is a testnet demo to showcase the capabilities of the system. Expect breaking changes, and the state may reset.

The private payment material is stored locally in your browser.

Without granting permanent storage, the browser can delete your local data under storage pressure or across updates.

Most of the data (all critical one like keys, secrets and notes) can be restored if it is lost.

The ASP provider needs your note public key and ASP secret to include you in the allowed list.

If you rely only on a public RPC and miss the event retention window, the app may need either a browser reminder to reopen the tab in time or a bootnode archive URL to rebuild history later.

You should understand the risks of using a bootnode (similar to the ones when using a 3d-party-hosted RPC:

  • Integrity risk: a bootnode can omit or forge event history.
  • Availability risk: a bootnode can be unavailable or rate limit you.
  • Privacy risk: the operator can observe IP address and timing.
  • Handoff risk: it can provide an incorrect ledger handoff point.
The UI uses a single explorer base URL across transaction feedback and address shortcuts.
Registration lets others transfer to your Stellar address without exchanging note and encryption public keys out of band.
